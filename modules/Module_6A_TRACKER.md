# Module 6A: Human Chronic SCI Pathology and Translational Imaging — Corpus Building Tracker

## Overview

This tracker monitors experiment-level and cohort-level evidence on human chronic spinal cord injury pathology, autopsy anatomy, translational imaging, tract integrity, lesion geometry, clinical trial screening anatomy, and pathology-imaging links.

**Target Corpus**: 20-40 primary human pathology/imaging papers + 8-15 reviews, with emphasis on lesion chronicity, tissue preservation/loss, tract integrity, motor/sensory linkage, and model-to-human translation.

## Scope

| Include | Exclude or Defer |
|---|---|
| Human autopsy, surgical, MRI, DTI, fMRI, MRS, and clinical imaging anatomy studies | Pure rehabilitation outcome papers without pathology/imaging anatomy |
| Chronic and longitudinal acute-to-chronic SCI imaging | Acute-only emergency imaging unless longitudinal chronic anatomy is included |
| Clinical trial screening papers that describe chronic lesion morphology | Animal-only imaging papers stay in mechanism modules unless translationally compared |
| Human tissue studies on motoneuron loss, remyelination, vascular pathology, cysts, scars, and spared tissue | Reviews only after primary search phases establish the map |
