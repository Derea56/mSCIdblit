# Module 15A: Peripheral Nerve and DRG Priming of CNS Repair - Corpus Building Tracker

## Overview

This tracker captures peripheral nerve conditioning, dorsal root ganglion (DRG) priming, and remote sensory-neuron regenerative-state induction as mechanisms that can enhance CNS axon regeneration and repair after spinal cord injury. The module focuses on the conditioning lesion paradigm, especially sciatic nerve injury before, at, or after dorsal column/spinal cord injury, and on DRG molecular programs that distinguish peripheral from central axotomy.

The module explicitly includes evidence that unilateral sciatic nerve injury can produce pro-regenerative changes in DRGs not directly associated with the injured nerve, including contralateral or remote segment effects. These rows should not be collapsed into generic axon-regeneration evidence in Module 5; Module 15 tracks the priming process, timing, DRG compartment, signaling axis, and central repair consequence.
