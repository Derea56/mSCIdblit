# Module 7A: Host Fibroinflammatory Conditioning Before Regenerative Reconstruction or Transplantation - Corpus Building Tracker

## Overview

This tracker monitors evidence that the recipient host's fibroinflammatory state at the time of implantation, tissue transfer, cell transplantation, or reconstructive surgery can determine downstream integration, fibrosis, vascularization, engraftment, regeneration, and functional recovery.

**Central hypothesis**: The success of transplantation, tissue engineering, and reconstructive surgery is determined in part by the recipient host's fibroinflammatory state at the time of implantation or tissue transfer.

**Primary research question**: Can systemic preoperative or perioperative host conditioning reduce fibrosis and improve constructive remodeling, graft integration, vascularization, engraftment, regeneration, or functional recovery after transplantation or reconstructive procedures?

**Module rule**: Module 7A is corpus-building and experiment-level extraction only. Do not create Module 7B consensus statements until the 7A corpus is saturated and observations, author claims, evidence links, and boundary records have been extracted separately.

**Emphasis**: Host preparation, not donor or graft preparation.

## Scope

| Include | Exclude or Defer |
|---|---|
| Systemic host conditioning started before implantation, immediately perioperatively, or with a clear perioperative rationale | Donor organ conditioning only |
| Oral, IV, SQ, inhaled, remote ischemic, biologic, or other systemic interventions targeting the recipient/host | Ex vivo cell or graft preconditioning without recipient intervention |
| Outcomes involving fibrosis, capsule formation, scar formation, vascularization, engraftment, tissue integration, regeneration, or functional recovery | Scaffold-local drug delivery only unless paired with systemic host conditioning |
| Reconstructive surgery, flap transfer, implant surgery, tissue engineering, regenerative medicine, cell transplantation, organ transplantation, SCI, wound healing, and foreign body response | Chronic immunosuppression begun only after transplant without a perioperative conditioning rationale |
| Studies that explicitly or implicitly condition the fibroinflammatory trajectory before reconstruction | Studies measuring only rejection without remodeling, tissue integration, fibrosis, vascularization, or repair outcomes |
