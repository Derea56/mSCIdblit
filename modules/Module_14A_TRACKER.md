# Module 14A: CRISPR-Based Epigenome Editing for Neuroregeneration - Corpus Building Tracker

## Overview

This tracker captures CRISPR-based epigenome editing as a programmable gene-regulatory strategy for neuroregeneration. The module focuses on catalytically inactive or nickase-impaired CRISPR systems used to activate, repress, methylate, demethylate, acetylate, deacetylate, or otherwise modify regulatory states without requiring permanent double-strand-break genome editing.

The neuroregeneration emphasis is specific: candidate uses include restoring neuron-intrinsic growth programs, repressing growth-inhibitory loci such as PTEN/KLF/SOCS family nodes, activating neurotrophic or regeneration-associated genes, targeting injury-responsive enhancers, and controlling glial or immune states that influence axon growth, repair, and lesion remodeling. Conventional CRISPR knockout, base editing, and prime editing are included only as boundary or comparator evidence when they define target genes, delivery constraints, or safety issues relevant to epigenome editing.
